When to Choose BJT vs FET

BJT (Bipolar Junction Transistor)

Best for audio amplification, radio circuits, Arduino projects, LED drivers, and low-frequency switching applications. Current-controlled devices with high gain.

FET / MOSFET

Best for high-speed switching, motor control, power supplies, DC-DC converters, and high-frequency circuits. Voltage-controlled with low gate power consumption.

Frequently Asked Questions

1. Do I need to match polarity (NPN vs PNP)?

Yes, absolutely. NPN and PNP transistors control current in opposite ways and cannot be swapped. Always replace an NPN with an NPN, and a PNP with a PNP.

2. Can I use a transistor with a higher VCEO rating?

Yes. The VCEO (Collector-Emitter Voltage) is the maximum voltage the transistor can handle when off. Using a part with a higher rating is safe and often recommended. Never use a lower rating, as it will cause breakdown.

3. What if the current rating (IC) is higher?

That's perfectly fine. A higher IC rating means the replacement can handle more current, which gives you a safety margin. Just ensure it's equal to or greater than your original part to avoid overheating.

4. Does the physical package and pinout matter?

Yes, very much. The replacement must physically fit on your circuit board (e.g., TO-92 for through-hole, SOT-23 for surface-mount). You must also verify the pinout (the order of Emitter, Base, and Collector pins) matches, or you'll need to modify your layout.
